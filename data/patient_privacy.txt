OVERVIEW

Patient privacy is a fundamental principle of healthcare. Healthcare organizations are responsible for protecting patient information and ensuring that health records are handled in a secure and confidential manner.

Patients have rights regarding how their health information is collected, stored, accessed, and shared. Healthcare organizations must implement safeguards to protect sensitive information from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or misuse.

DEFINITION OF HEALTH INFORMATION

Health information includes any information related to a person's:

* Medical history
* Diagnoses
* Treatments
* Medications
* Laboratory results
* Imaging reports
* Appointment records
* Insurance information
* Billing information

Health information may exist in paper records, electronic systems, or other approved formats.

PATIENT PRIVACY RIGHTS

Patients generally have the right to:

* Access their health records
* Request corrections to inaccurate information
* Receive information about privacy practices
* Request limitations on certain disclosures
* Obtain copies of their records when permitted
* Request confidential communications when available

Healthcare organizations may establish procedures for processing such requests.

ACCESS TO HEALTH RECORDS

Access to patient records is limited to authorized individuals who require the information to perform approved healthcare, operational, or administrative functions.

Examples of authorized access may include:

* Physicians
* Nurses
* Pharmacists
* Laboratory personnel
* Billing staff
* Approved administrative personnel

Access should be limited to the minimum information necessary to perform assigned responsibilities.

UNAUTHORIZED ACCESS

Unauthorized access occurs when an individual views, uses, or discloses patient information without appropriate authorization or legitimate business need.

Examples include:

* Viewing records out of curiosity
* Sharing patient information without authorization
* Accessing records unrelated to assigned duties
* Using patient information for personal purposes

Unauthorized access may result in disciplinary action and other consequences.

DISCLOSURE OF HEALTH INFORMATION

Healthcare organizations may disclose health information in situations permitted by law, policy, or patient authorization.

Examples may include:

* Treatment coordination
* Healthcare operations
* Payment processing
* Public health reporting
* Legal requirements
* Patient-authorized disclosures

Disclosure practices are subject to applicable regulations and organizational policies.

PATIENT AUTHORIZATION

Certain disclosures may require patient authorization before information can be released.

Authorization requests may include:

* Purpose of disclosure
* Information being released
* Recipient of information
* Duration of authorization

Patients may revoke authorization when permitted by applicable policies and regulations.

MINIMUM NECESSARY STANDARD

Healthcare personnel should access or disclose only the minimum amount of information necessary to complete a specific task.

Examples include:

* Billing personnel accessing billing-related information
* Scheduling staff accessing appointment information
* Providers accessing clinical information needed for treatment

Limiting information access helps protect patient privacy.

ELECTRONIC HEALTH RECORDS

Electronic health records are commonly used to store and manage patient information.

Healthcare organizations should implement safeguards such as:

* User authentication
* Password protection
* Access controls
* Audit logging
* Encryption when appropriate
* Security monitoring

Electronic systems should be regularly maintained and reviewed for security risks.

PATIENT PORTALS

Patient portals allow patients to access portions of their health information electronically.

Portal features may include:

* Viewing appointments
* Accessing test results
* Requesting prescription refills
* Secure messaging
* Reviewing visit summaries

Patients are encouraged to protect account credentials and use secure devices when accessing portal services.

CONFIDENTIAL COMMUNICATIONS

Patients may request confidential methods of communication when available.

Examples include:

* Alternative mailing addresses
* Secure electronic communications
* Designated contact preferences

Organizations may establish procedures for managing such requests.

DATA SECURITY RESPONSIBILITIES

Healthcare organizations should take reasonable steps to protect information from:

* Unauthorized access
* Data breaches
* Theft
* Loss
* Improper disclosure
* Cybersecurity threats

Security measures may include administrative, technical, and physical safeguards.

BREACH RESPONSE

A privacy or security breach may occur when protected information is accessed, used, or disclosed improperly.

Organizations may respond by:

* Investigating the incident
* Containing the exposure
* Assessing potential impact
* Implementing corrective actions
* Providing required notifications

Response procedures depend on organizational policies and applicable regulations.

PATIENT RESPONSIBILITIES

Patients can help protect their information by:

* Keeping passwords secure
* Updating contact information
* Monitoring account activity
* Protecting personal devices
* Reporting suspicious activity

Shared responsibility helps improve information security.

RETENTION OF HEALTH RECORDS

Healthcare organizations may retain records for periods established by applicable laws, regulations, and organizational policies.

Retention periods may vary depending on:

* Record type
* Patient age
* Regulatory requirements
* Legal obligations

Records should be managed in accordance with approved retention schedules.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: Who can access my health records?
A: Access is generally limited to authorized individuals who require the information for approved healthcare, operational, or administrative purposes.

Q: Can my health information be shared without my permission?
A: Certain disclosures may be permitted by law, policy, or healthcare operations. Other disclosures may require authorization.

Q: Can I request a copy of my records?
A: Patients may request access to their records according to organizational procedures and applicable regulations.

Q: What should I do if I suspect unauthorized access?
A: Patients should report concerns to the appropriate healthcare organization or privacy office.

Q: Are electronic health records secure?
A: Healthcare organizations implement security measures to protect electronic records, although no system can completely eliminate risk.
